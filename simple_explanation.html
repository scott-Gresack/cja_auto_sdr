Think of these two tools like a doctor's assistant and a medical record keeper:

Both help companies understand how people use their products and services - but they do it in different ways.

Let me explain each one...

üè• AEP Health Viewer

What it does in one sentence: It watches your customer data system to make sure everything is working correctly - right now.

Think of it like a HOSPITAL MONITOR that watches a patient's heartbeat, breathing, and blood pressure in real-time.

Real-Life Example:

A big online store uses this to watch their customer tracking system. If a customer's information isn't being collected properly, the monitor shows a RED LIGHT and alerts the team immediately. Just like a hospital alarm!

What It Actually Does:

  • Checks if customer information is being recorded correctly
  • Shows which parts are working and which are broken
  • Alerts you instantly if something goes wrong
  • Helps fix problems before customers notice
  • Shows real-time data on a dashboard (like a video game scoreboard)

Who Needs It?

Companies that need to know RIGHT NOW if their customer tracking is working. (If you have a website or app, you probably need this!)

üìã CJA SDR Generator

What it does in one sentence: It automatically creates a detailed instruction manual for your customer data collection - showing exactly what you're tracking and why.

Think of it like an ENCYCLOPEDIA that documents everything in your library - what books you have, where they are, and how they're organized.

Real-Life Example:

When a new manager joins a big company, they ask: "What customer information are we collecting? How many pieces? What does each piece mean?" This tool reads all that information and creates a perfect instruction book - automatically, in seconds.

What It Actually Does:

  • Looks at all your customer data collection setup
  • Reads every metric and dimension (fancy words for "things being measured")
  • Checks the data for quality issues
  • Creates an Excel spreadsheet with all the details
  • Tracks what changed compared to last time

Who Needs It?

Companies that need to document what they're tracking for their team, audits, or compliance requirements. (Usually once a month or when something changes.)
